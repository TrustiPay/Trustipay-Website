Domain

Domain details gathered from the literature survey

TrustiPay studies the combined problem of digital payment accessibility, fraud exposure, unstable connectivity, and the limited decision value of raw transaction records.

Literature Survey

Digital payment systems improve financial inclusion, but voice-enabled financial systems often lack safety-bounded execution and multilingual support for low-resource languages. Existing fraud detection research uses supervised and anomaly-based machine learning, but real deployments still face class imbalance, concept drift, and false positives. Offline payment research supports device-level processing, local ledgers, and delayed synchronization, but must handle ordering, replay resistance, and double-spend prevention. Financial analytics systems can visualize spending, but many still provide fragmented summaries instead of actionable financial guidance.

Research Gap

Prior work usually treats accessibility, fraud detection, offline payments, and analytics as separate areas. TrustiPay addresses the architectural gap by coordinating them inside one shared transaction lifecycle.

Research Problem

Users need a digital payment platform that remains usable, trustworthy, and secure when language barriers, visual or age-related accessibility needs, fraud risk, and poor connectivity occur together.

Research Objectives

  • Design a safe bilingual voice-assisted payment experience where sensitive actions still require explicit confirmation.
  • Build a hybrid fraud decision service that maps risk into allow, OTP challenge, or block actions.
  • Support offline peer-to-peer transfers through a tamper-evident local ledger and deferred fraud review after synchronization.
  • Convert confirmed transaction histories into interpretable financial health scores, behavioral profiles, and personalized guidance.

Methodology

The project follows a prototype-oriented systems methodology. The Android client manages wallet interaction, confirmation, voice guidance, and temporary local storage. Modular FastAPI services handle fraud and risk evaluation, transaction orchestration with offline support, and financial analytics.

Technologies Used

  • Android client
  • FastAPI backend
  • SQLite local deployment
  • Isolation Forest
  • Random Forest
  • OTP verification
  • QR, Wi-Fi Direct, Bluetooth
  • Hash-linked ledger

Platform summary

One lifecycle for interaction, risk, continuity, and insight

01

Guided interaction

Voice guidance assists navigation and parameter prefilling, while transaction commitment remains protected by explicit confirmation.

02

Adaptive fraud decisioning

Anomaly-aware scoring and supervised classification produce a risk state that becomes allow, OTP challenge, or block.

03

Controlled offline execution

Disconnected payments are stored locally, synchronized later, and finalized only after signature checks, duplicate handling, ordering checks, and fraud review.

04

Financial guidance

Confirmed transactions feed financial health scoring, behavior profiles, top contributing factors, and personalized recommendations.
