Domain

Domain details gathered from the literature survey

TrustiPay studies the combined problem of digital payment accessibility, fraud exposure, unstable connectivity, and the limited decision value of raw transaction records in emerging-economy payment environments.

Research focus

Integrated trust layer for inclusive digital payments

Most payment applications optimize transaction speed, but TrustiPay focuses on the broader conditions that determine whether people can confidently use digital payments: language accessibility, safe voice guidance, risk-aware transaction approval, offline transaction continuity, and useful financial feedback after payments are completed.

01

Literature Survey

Digital payment platforms have improved financial inclusion by making transactions faster and more convenient. However, the literature shows that adoption is still affected by accessibility barriers, especially for visually impaired users, elderly users, and users who need local-language assistance. Voice-enabled systems can reduce interaction difficulty, but they must not allow sensitive financial actions to be committed without strong confirmation.

Fraud detection research has widely used anomaly detection and supervised machine learning. These methods can identify suspicious transaction patterns, but practical payment environments still face class imbalance, concept drift, contextual behavior changes, and false-positive decisions that frustrate legitimate users. TrustiPay therefore treats fraud detection as policy-based transaction decisioning instead of a standalone prediction result.

Offline payment research shows the value of local ledgers, device-level processing, and delayed synchronization. Yet offline systems must still handle duplicate records, replay attempts, transaction ordering, double-spend risks, and integrity validation. Financial analytics research also shows that raw transaction summaries are not enough; users benefit more from interpretable financial indicators and personalized recommendations.

02

Research Gap

Existing work usually improves one area at a time: accessibility, fraud detection, offline payments, or spending analytics. The gap is the lack of a unified architecture where these capabilities work together inside the same transaction lifecycle. Without integration, accessibility may introduce unsafe automation, offline continuity may bypass risk controls, and analytics may be disconnected from confirmed transaction states.

03

Research Problem

How can a digital payment platform support users who face language, accessibility, fraud, and connectivity challenges while still maintaining transaction safety, ledger integrity, and practical financial guidance? TrustiPay addresses this problem by coordinating user interaction, risk scoring, offline synchronization, and analytics through one safety-bounded flow.

04

Research Objectives

  • Design a bilingual voice-assisted payment experience that improves accessibility while keeping sensitive actions under explicit user confirmation.
  • Create a hybrid fraud decision pipeline that combines anomaly-aware scoring, supervised learning, and contextual behavioral signals.
  • Map transaction risk into practical outcomes: allow low-risk transactions, challenge medium-risk transactions with OTP verification, and block high-risk transactions.
  • Enable offline peer-to-peer payment continuity using tamper-evident local storage, unique transaction identifiers, signatures, and hash-linked ledger entries.
  • Synchronize offline transactions through deferred confirmation so that offline records still pass duplicate handling, ordering checks, integrity validation, and fraud review.
  • Transform confirmed transaction histories into financial health scores, behavior profiles, contributing factors, and personalized recommendations.
05

Methodology

This research follows a prototype-oriented systems methodology. The TrustiPay Android client supports wallet interaction, transaction confirmation, bilingual guidance, and temporary offline storage. Backend responsibilities are separated into modular FastAPI service domains so that fraud scoring, ledger synchronization, and analytics can be tested independently while still operating in one end-to-end lifecycle.

Online pathVoice guidance prepares transactions, explicit confirmation protects commitment, and fraud scoring occurs before final approval.
Offline pathDisconnected transfers are captured locally, exchanged through peer-to-peer methods, synchronized later, and finalized only after review.
Analytics pathOnly confirmed transaction history is used to generate financial health indicators, behavior profiles, and recommendations.

07

Expected Contribution

TrustiPay contributes an implementation-driven architecture for inclusive and security-aware digital payments. Its main contribution is the coordination of guided interaction, adaptive fraud handling, offline transaction resilience, and financial decision support rather than treating these capabilities as isolated add-ons.

Platform summary

One lifecycle for interaction, risk, continuity, and insight

01

Guided interaction

Voice guidance assists navigation and parameter prefilling, while transaction commitment remains protected by explicit confirmation.

02

Adaptive fraud decisioning

Anomaly-aware scoring and supervised classification produce a risk state that becomes allow, OTP challenge, or block.

03

Controlled offline execution

Disconnected payments are stored locally, synchronized later, and finalized only after signature checks, duplicate handling, ordering checks, and fraud review.

04

Financial guidance

Confirmed transactions feed financial health scoring, behavior profiles, top contributing factors, and personalized recommendations.
